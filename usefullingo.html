Learn AI Lingo

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI): A field of computer science focused on creating systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence (e.g., reasoning, language understanding, vision, problem-solving).
  • Machine Learning (ML): A subset of AI where systems learn patterns from data instead of being explicitly programmed. The system improves performance as it processes more examples.
  • Deep Learning: A type of machine learning that uses many-layered neural networks to learn complex patterns—commonly used in image recognition, speech processing, and language models.
  • Neural Network: A computational model inspired by the human brain, made up of interconnected “neurons” (nodes) that process and pass information.
  • Large Language Model (LLM): A type of AI trained on vast amounts of text to understand and generate human-like language. (Example: ChatGPT)
  • Training Data: The information fed into an AI system during training to help it learn patterns, relationships, and behaviors.
  • Algorithm: A set of rules or instructions a computer follows to solve a problem or perform a task.
  • Dataset: A collection of data (text, images, audio, numbers, etc.) used to train or evaluate machine learning models.
  • Prompt: Text (or other input) given to an AI model to tell it what you want it to generate or answer.
  • Token: A small unit of text—often a word or piece of a word—that AI models process and generate.
  • Inference: The process of an AI model using what it has learned to produce an output (e.g., answering a question).
  • Overfitting: When a model learns the training data too well—including its noise and mistakes—so it performs poorly on new, unseen data.
  • Bias (in AI): Systematic errors in an AI model caused by unbalanced or flawed training data, leading to unfair or inaccurate outputs.
  • Fine-Tuning: Training an existing AI model further on a smaller, specific dataset to specialize it for particular tasks.
  • Reinforcement Learning: A type of learning where an AI system improves by receiving rewards or penalties based on its actions—similar to training through trial and error.
  • API (Application Programming Interface): A tool that allows developers to connect AI capabilities to apps or services.
  • Model Parameters: The internal values a model adjusts during training to learn patterns. Large models have billions of parameters.
  • Multimodal AI: AI systems that can process multiple types of data—e.g., text, images, audio, video—simultaneously.
  • Generative AI: AI that can create new content such as text, images, music, or code based on what it has learned.
  • Hallucination (in AI): When an AI produces information that sounds plausible but is factually incorrect or made up.
  • AI Slop: an informal, often humorous or critical term used to describe low-quality, bland, repetitive, or obviously machine-generated content produced by AI systems.
